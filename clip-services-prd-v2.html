01Executive Summary

Clip Services is a hyper-local two-sided service marketplace operating from Clippers Quays, Salford. The platform connects residents, apartment dwellers, and businesses in the Salford Quays / MediaCityUK area with verified local service providers — runners, cleaners, handymen, dog walkers, beauty technicians, tech helpers, and more.

Unlike global platforms (TaskRabbit, Bark.com), Clip Services is hyper-local by design. Every provider is from the area. Every customer is within 3 miles. The founder — Sonia — manages the platform, vets all providers, and earns a 15% commission on every booking without personally delivering any service.

Core model: Sonia runs the platform. Local providers deliver the services. Customers pay through Clip Services. Sonia keeps 15% of every transaction automatically.

Sonia does not deliver services. Her role is platform operator:

Review provider applications — Vet every applicant via WhatsApp, confirm identity, check experience, approve or decline.
Manage the listings — Add approved providers to the website. Update ratings after jobs. Remove underperforming providers.

03Problem Statement

Customer problems

No trusted local directory. Finding a reliable cleaner, handyman, or dog walker near Clippers Quays means using Google (unreliable), Bark.com (national, expensive, slow), or word of mouth (limited).
No vetted providers. Booking a stranger from Facebook Marketplace or Gumtree carries real safety and quality risk. Customers have no protection.
No single platform for all local services. Cleaning, running, dog walking, beauty — currently requires 4 different apps or platforms.
No local payment protection. Most local service transactions are cash-in-hand with no receipt and no recourse if the job is bad.

Provider problems

No customers. Freelance cleaners, runners, dog walkers, and beauty technicians in Salford struggle to find clients outside their personal network.
No credibility platform. An individual with 3 years of cleaning experience has no way to showcase reviews and ratings to new customers.
Chasing payments. Cash-in-hand work means providers often wait, follow up, or go unpaid after completed jobs.
No marketing budget. Most local freelancers can't afford Facebook ads or Google listings. They rely entirely on referrals.

06User Stories

Customer stories

As a busy apartment resident, I want to browse verified local service providers, so that I can book someone trustworthy without searching Facebook or Google.
As a customer without a car, I want to filter providers by service type, so that I can find exactly who I need in under 30 seconds.
As a first-time user, I want to see provider ratings and reviews, so that I can feel confident booking someone I've never met.
As a business customer, I want to book a provider via WhatsApp or the website form, so that I don't have to create an account or learn a new platform.

Provider stories

As a freelance cleaner, I want to apply to join the platform in under 5 minutes, so that I can start receiving bookings without a complicated signup.
As a service provider, I want to set my own rates, so that Clip Services doesn't undercut what I already charge my existing clients.
As a provider with no online presence, I want a verified profile with real reviews, so that customers trust me before they've ever met me.
As a busy provider, I want booking notifications via WhatsApp, so that I don't have to log into a platform to manage my schedule.
As a provider who hates chasing payment, I want Clip Services to collect and transfer my earnings automatically, so that I never have to invoice or follow up again.

Platform owner stories (Sonia)

As the platform owner, I want all provider applications submitted via a form, so that I can review, verify, and approve providers at my own pace.
As the platform owner, I want to earn 15% commission on every booking automatically, so that the business generates income without me delivering any service personally.
As the platform owner, I want to be able to feature or remove providers, so that I maintain quality and protect the platform's reputation.

07User Flows

Customer booking flow

1. Lands on website — sees "I need a service" tab active by default
2. Searches or filters — types service or clicks category pill (e.g. Cleaning)
3. Browses provider cards — sees name, rating, services, price, availability
4. Clicks "View & Book" — modal opens with full profile, reviews, booking form
5. Fills booking form — name, WhatsApp, service needed, date/time, address
6. Submits request — Sonia receives it via form notification
7. Sonia confirms via WhatsApp — sends price breakdown and payment link within 30 mins
8. Customer pays — Monzo, bank transfer, or Stripe link
9. Provider notified — Sonia WhatsApps provider with job details
10. Job completed — Sonia transfers 85% to provider. Keeps 15%.
11. Review requested — Sonia messages customer for a rating within 24hrs

Provider application flow

1. Clicks "I offer a service" tab — sees provider hero, benefits, how it works
2. Scrolls to application form — fills in: name, WhatsApp, services, rate, availability, experience
3. Submits application — Sonia receives via form notification (email + WhatsApp)
4. Sonia reviews within 48hrs — checks experience, rate reasonableness, service fit
5. Sonia WhatsApps applicant — brief call or voice note verification
6. Provider approved — Sonia manually adds provider card to website
7. Provider goes live — visible to all customers immediately
8. First booking arrives — Sonia notifies provider via WhatsApp with full job details

Data handling rules

Minimise data collection. Only collect what's needed: name, WhatsApp, email, address (for booking only), service type. Never store payment card details — use Monzo links or Stripe.
Limit data sharing. Never share a customer's full address with a provider until the booking is confirmed and paid. Share only first name, not full name, in initial provider notification.
Google Sheets security. Enable 2FA on the Google account. Don't share the master sheet link publicly. If a VA is hired, give view-only access to the columns they need.
Right to deletion. If a customer or provider asks to be removed, delete their data from all sheets within 30 days (GDPR requirement).
WhatsApp chat retention. Keep booking-related chats for 12 months (evidence for disputes). Delete personal conversations that aren't service-related.

Safety protocols

Provider ID verification. Before approval, verify government-issued photo ID via WhatsApp video call. Store a record that verification was completed (not the ID itself).
In-home service safety. For cleaning, handyman, and beauty: require DBS check, share provider's full name and photo with customer before the visit, and check in with customer via WhatsApp after the job.
Emergency protocol. If a customer reports feeling unsafe during a service, Sonia calls the provider immediately to end the job, then contacts the customer directly. If serious, advise calling 999. Document everything.
Three-strike policy. First complaint = investigation + warning. Second complaint = temporary suspension + mandatory conversation. Third complaint = permanent removal from platform.

19Go-to-Market Strategy

The marketplace cold-start problem is the hardest challenge. Customers won't come without providers. Providers won't come without customers. The strategy below solves supply first, then uses supply to attract demand.

Phase 1 — Seed the supply side (Week 1–2)

Personal recruitment. Sonia personally recruits 5 founding providers from her existing network or local community groups. These are people she already knows or can vet in person. They understand they're early and may have few bookings initially.
Founding provider incentive. First 5 providers pay 0% commission for their first month. This gets them on the platform with zero risk. After Month 1, standard 15% applies. Creates urgency to apply early.
Category coverage. Ensure the first 5 providers cover at least 4 categories: Runner, Cleaning, Handyman, Dog Walking. A marketplace with only one service type doesn't feel like a marketplace.

Phase 2 — Create demand (Week 2–4)

Flyer drop — Clippers Quays. 50 flyers in lobby, mailboxes, common areas, and gym noticeboard. QR code links to website. Headline: "Your neighbours are already using Clip Services." Simple, local, immediate.

20Strategic Recommendations

These recommendations address gaps in the current plan and suggest high-impact improvements to strengthen the marketplace, reduce risk, and accelerate growth.

Pre-launch critical actions

Draft provider agreement before first approval
A simple 1-page agreement confirming: self-employed status, 15% commission terms, right to remove listing, requirement for own insurance (where applicable), data handling consent. This protects Sonia legally from day one. Use a free template from GOV.UK and adapt it.
Critical
Add Terms of Service and Privacy Policy pages to website
Required by law (GDPR, Consumer Rights Act 2015). A privacy policy must explain what data is collected and how. Terms must define Clip Services as a marketplace facilitator, not an employer or service provider. Free templates available from ICO and Termly.
Critical
Register with ICO as a data controller
Processing personal data (names, addresses, phone numbers) requires ICO registration. Costs £40/year. Failure to register can result in fines. Takes 10 minutes online at ico.org.uk.
Critical
Set up dedicated Monzo Business account
Separate all Clip Services income and expenses from personal finances. Makes tax reporting clean, commission tracking automatic, and provider payments traceable. Essential if forming a Ltd company later.

Growth accelerators

Launch a referral programme from day one
Offer both sides an incentive: customers get £5 off their next booking for each friend referred. Providers get featured placement for 1 week for each provider they refer. The cheapest customer acquisition channel for a local marketplace is word of mouth — but it needs a nudge.

Build a "Clip Regulars" loyalty programme
After 5 bookings, customers unlock a 5% discount on all future bookings (Sonia absorbs this from her 15%, so provider still gets full rate). Encourages repeat usage and increases LTV. Simple to track in Google Sheets.

Bookings requested within 2 hours carry a 25% platform fee instead of 15%. Provider still gets their full rate — Sonia keeps the premium. Customers are willing to pay more for urgency. Provider receives a "same-day bonus" from the extra 10%.

Partner with property management companies
Clippers Quays, NV Buildings, The Quays — approach building management to become the recommended services provider for residents. Feature in welcome packs, lobby screens, and resident WhatsApp groups. One partnership = instant access to hundreds of potential customers.

Platform resilience

Reduce WhatsApp single-point-of-failure
WhatsApp is currently the backbone of operations. If the account gets restricted, the business stops. Collect email addresses for all customers and providers. Set up a simple email notification system (e.g. Mailchimp free tier) as a fallback. Consider WhatsApp Business API by Month 3 for higher message volumes and reduced ban risk.
P2
Create a provider WhatsApp community
Group all approved providers into a WhatsApp Community channel. Post available jobs, share tips, celebrate milestones ("Amara just completed 50 jobs!"). Builds provider loyalty and reduces churn. Providers who feel part of a community are less likely to leave for a competitor or go direct.
Launch
Track unit economics from month one
Add a simple "Unit Economics" tab to the Google Sheets tracker: CAC per customer, LTV per customer, commission per booking, average bookings per provider per week, provider churn rate. These numbers tell Sonia whether the business is healthy before revenue alone does.
Launch
Plan the Stripe Connect migration early
Manual payment splits won't scale past ~50 bookings/week. Begin Stripe Connect onboarding research in Month 1. By Month 3, have it ready to deploy. When it launches, position it as an upgrade: "Providers now get paid automatically within 24 hours — no waiting for manual transfers."
V2

Risks to watch closely

The #1 existential risk is not competition — it's provider quality. One bad experience shared on a local Facebook group or TikTok can destroy trust in a hyper-local community faster than any marketing campaign can rebuild it. Invest disproportionately in vetting, DBS checks, and post-job follow-ups. The provider approval rate should be 50–60%, not 90%. Saying "no" to bad providers protects the brand.

The #2 risk is Sonia burning out. At 50+ bookings/week with manual payments, manual provider coordination, and manual review collection — the workload becomes unsustainable. The roadmap already plans for Stripe automation and a part-time assistant, but these need to happen at Month 2, not Month 4. Build for delegation from day one.
