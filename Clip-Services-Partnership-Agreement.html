3. Purpose of This Agreement

This Agreement sets out the terms under which the Partners agree to carry on the Business together, including the division of ownership, responsibilities, profit sharing, decision-making authority, and the rights and obligations of each Partner.

This Agreement is legally binding upon execution by both parties and shall remain in effect until terminated in accordance with the provisions set out herein.

4. Ownership & Equity Split

  1. Party A shall hold seventy-five percent (75%) equity interest in the Business.
  2. Party B shall hold twenty-five percent (25%) equity interest in the Business.
  3. These percentages represent each Partner's proportional ownership of the Business, its assets (including intellectual property, brand, code, domain names, and customer data), and its liabilities.
  4. No Partner may sell, transfer, pledge, or assign any portion of their equity interest without the prior written consent of the other Partner (see Clause 13).

6. Vesting Schedule

Party B's 25% equity interest shall vest over a period of [12 / 18 / 24] months from the Effective Date, as follows:

  1. Cliff period: No equity shall vest during the first [3 / 6] months ("the Cliff Period"). If Party B exits or is removed during the Cliff Period, they forfeit all equity and receive no ownership interest.
  2. Post-cliff vesting: After the Cliff Period, equity shall vest monthly on a pro-rata basis. For example, if the vesting period is 24 months with a 6-month cliff, Party B vests approximately 1.39% per month for the remaining 18 months.
  3. Full vesting: Upon completion of the full vesting period, Party B shall hold the full 25% equity interest.
  4. Acceleration: Vesting may be accelerated at Party A's sole discretion, or automatically upon the sale of the Business (see Clause 15).
Why vesting matters: This protects both parties. If Party B leaves after 2 months, they don't walk away with 25% of a business they barely contributed to. It ensures commitment.

7. Profit & Loss Sharing

  1. Net profits (revenue minus all business expenses, taxes, and reinvestment) shall be distributed in proportion to each Partner's vested equity:
    • Party A: 75%
    • Party B: 25% (of vested portion only)
  2. Net losses shall be shared in the same proportions.
  3. Profit distributions shall be made [monthly / quarterly], within 14 days of the end of each period.
  4. The Partners may mutually agree to reinvest a portion of profits back into the Business instead of distributing them. Such agreement must be documented in writing.
  5. Each Partner is individually responsible for their own tax obligations (Income Tax, National Insurance) arising from their share of the profits. The Business shall not be responsible for paying either Partner's personal tax.

9. Decision-Making & Authority

9.1 Day-to-Day Decisions

Each Partner may make routine operational decisions within their area of responsibility without consulting the other, provided such decisions do not involve expenditure exceeding £[250 / 500] or materially alter the Business model.

9.2 Major Decisions (Require Both Partners' Consent)

The following decisions require the written agreement of both Partners:

  • Any single expenditure or commitment exceeding £[500 / 1,000]
  • Hiring or engaging employees, contractors, or consultants
  • Taking on business debt, loans, or credit facilities
  • Changing the Business name, brand, or domain
  • Entering into partnerships, joint ventures, or affiliations with third parties
  • Changing the pricing model or platform fee structure
  • Expanding the Business to new geographical areas

9.3 Founder Veto (Reserved Matters)

Party A retains sole and final authority over the following matters, regardless of Party B's consent:

  • Sale, merger, or dissolution of the Business
  • Issuing additional equity to new partners or investors
  • Changes to the technology platform, codebase, or infrastructure
  • Conversion to a Limited Company (timing and structure)
  • Appointing or removing directors (upon incorporation)

10. Financial Management & Banking

  1. The Business shall maintain a dedicated business bank account, separate from either Partner's personal accounts.
  2. Party A shall be the primary signatory and administrator of all business bank accounts and payment platforms (including Stripe).
  3. Party B shall have read-only access to financial records and bank statements for transparency.
  4. Both Partners shall maintain accurate records of all income, expenses, and distributions.
  5. A financial summary shall be shared between both Partners on a [weekly / monthly] basis.
  6. Neither Partner shall use business funds for personal expenses.

11. Intellectual Property

  1. All intellectual property ("IP") created before the Effective Date of this Agreement — including but not limited to the Clip Services brand, logo, website, codebase, designs, and business processes — is and shall remain the sole property of Party A.
  2. Any IP created jointly by both Partners after the Effective Date shall be owned by the Business in proportion to each Partner's equity share.
  3. Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason, Party A retains full ownership and control of all IP listed in Clause 11(a). Party B's interest in jointly-created IP (Clause 11(b)) shall be determined by their vested equity at the time of exit.
  4. Neither Partner may register trademarks, domains, or other IP related to Clip Services in their personal name without the other's written consent.

12. Confidentiality & Data Protection

  1. Both Partners agree to keep confidential all non-public information relating to the Business, including but not limited to: financial records, customer data, provider data, trade secrets, business strategies, partnerships, and proprietary technology.
  2. This obligation of confidentiality shall survive the termination of this Agreement for a period of 3 (three) years.
  3. Both Partners shall comply with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018 in respect of all personal data processed by the Business.
  4. Neither Partner shall access, copy, or export customer or provider data for personal use or for use in any other business.

13. Non-Compete & Non-Solicitation

13.1 Non-Compete

During the term of this Agreement and for [12 / 18 / 24] months following its termination, neither Partner shall directly or indirectly:

  • Own, operate, manage, or be employed by any business that competes with Clip Services within a [10 / 25 / 50]-mile radius of Salford Quays
  • Develop, launch, or invest in a competing service marketplace platform targeting the same customer base

13.2 Non-Solicitation

For [12 / 24] months after termination, neither Partner shall:

  • Solicit, recruit, or attempt to recruit any service provider listed on the Clip Services platform
  • Solicit or attempt to divert any customer of the Business
  • Encourage any employee, contractor, or partner of the Business to terminate their relationship with the Business

14. Breach, Disputes & Remedies

14.1 Material Breach

A material breach of this Agreement includes, but is not limited to:

  • Failure to meet the minimum time commitment for 30+ consecutive days without prior agreement
  • Misappropriation of business funds
  • Breach of confidentiality obligations
  • Breach of non-compete or non-solicitation clauses
  • Conduct that brings the Business into disrepute
  • Fraud, dishonesty, or criminal activity

14.2 Remediation Process

  1. The non-breaching Partner shall provide written notice of the breach, specifying the nature of the breach and a reasonable period (not less than 14 days) to remedy it.
  2. If the breach is not remedied within the specified period, the non-breaching Partner may invoke the exit provisions in Clause 15.

14.3 Dispute Resolution

  1. Negotiation: The Partners shall first attempt to resolve any dispute through good-faith negotiation within 14 days.
  2. Mediation: If negotiation fails, the dispute shall be referred to an independent mediator agreed upon by both Partners. Mediation costs shall be shared equally.
  3. Legal proceedings: If mediation fails, either Partner may pursue legal proceedings under the jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.

15. Exit, Buyout & Dissolution

15.1 Voluntary Exit

Either Partner may exit the Business by providing [60 / 90] days written notice to the other Partner. Upon exit:

  1. The remaining Partner has the right of first refusal to buy the exiting Partner's vested equity at fair market value, to be determined by an independent valuation or a formula agreed upon in Schedule B.
  2. If the remaining Partner does not exercise this right within 30 days, the exiting Partner may seek an external buyer, subject to the remaining Partner's approval (which shall not be unreasonably withheld).
  3. Any unvested equity at the time of exit is automatically forfeited.

15.2 Removal for Breach

If a Partner is removed due to material breach (Clause 14), the non-breaching Partner may purchase the breaching Partner's vested equity at a 50% discount to fair market value, as a penalty for breach. Unvested equity is forfeited entirely.

15.3 Death or Incapacity

In the event of a Partner's death or permanent incapacity, their vested equity interest shall pass to their designated beneficiary or estate. The surviving Partner shall have the right of first refusal to purchase such interest at fair market value within 90 days.

15.4 Dissolution

The Business may only be dissolved by the mutual written agreement of both Partners. Upon dissolution:

  1. All business debts and obligations shall be settled first.
  2. Remaining assets shall be distributed in proportion to each Partner's vested equity.
  3. Intellectual property created before the Effective Date reverts solely to Party A (Clause 11).

16. Salaries & Drawings

  1. Neither Partner shall receive a fixed salary unless mutually agreed in writing and the Business can sustain it.
  2. Partners may take drawings (advance payments against their profit share) up to a maximum of £[AMOUNT] per month, subject to the Business maintaining a minimum cash reserve of £[AMOUNT].
  3. All drawings must be recorded and reconciled against profit distributions at the end of each [month / quarter].

17. Liability

  1. As a sole trader / partnership, both Partners acknowledge that they have unlimited personal liability for the debts and obligations of the Business.
  2. Neither Partner shall incur debts, enter into contracts, or create obligations on behalf of the Business that exceed £[500 / 1,000] without the prior written consent of the other Partner.
  3. Each Partner shall indemnify the other against any loss, damage, or liability arising from their own negligence, fraud, or unauthorised actions.
Important: Converting to a Limited Company (Clause 18) will provide limited liability protection for both Partners. Until then, both parties are personally liable. This is a significant legal risk to be aware of.

18. Conversion to Limited Company

  1. Both Partners agree that the Business shall be converted to a Limited Company registered with Companies House when commercially appropriate, at the sole discretion of Party A.
  2. Upon incorporation, shares shall be issued reflecting each Partner's vested equity at the time of conversion (e.g. 75 ordinary shares to Party A, up to 25 ordinary shares to Party B based on vesting).
  3. This Agreement shall be superseded by a formal Shareholders' Agreement upon incorporation, which shall reflect the same principles and protections set out herein.
  4. Both Partners agree to cooperate fully with the incorporation process, including signing all necessary documents.

19. Anti-Dilution Protection

If the Business takes on additional partners or investors after the Effective Date:

  1. Party B's equity shall not be diluted below [20% / 15%] without Party B's written consent.
  2. Any new equity issuance shall dilute both Partners proportionally unless otherwise agreed.
  3. Party B shall have pro-rata rights to participate in any future investment round to maintain their percentage.

20. Insurance

The Business shall obtain and maintain the following insurance policies as soon as commercially practical:

  • Public Liability Insurance
  • Professional Indemnity Insurance
  • Cyber Liability Insurance (given the online nature of the platform)

The cost of insurance shall be borne by the Business as an operating expense.

21. Amendments

This Agreement may only be amended, modified, or supplemented by a written document signed by both Partners. Verbal agreements or understandings shall have no legal effect.

22. Entire Agreement

23. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales. Both Partners submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.

24. Severability
