02Pre-Launch — Critical Tasks

These items must be completed before sharing the site with real customers or providers. Launching without them creates legal, trust, or data-loss risk.

Replace placeholder WhatsApp number with real number
Currently set to +447700000000 in the WhatsApp floating button, footer links, and all "wa.me" href attributes. Update to Sonia's actual WhatsApp Business number.
Critical Sonia 10 min
Connect booking form to backend (email / Google Sheets / API)
Currently, submitting the booking form shows a toast but data goes nowhere. Options: (a) send form data to Google Sheets via Apps Script, (b) email via Formspree/EmailJS, (c) use existing Vercel KV API at /api/booking. Pick one and wire it up.
Critical Dev 2–3 hrs
Connect provider application form to backend
Same issue as booking form — application data is lost on submit. Wire to Google Sheets or Vercel KV so Sonia can review applications. Include all fields: name, WhatsApp, email, postcode, services, rate, experience, availability, transport, referral source.
Critical Dev 2–3 hrs
Add Privacy Policy page
Required by GDPR before collecting any personal data. Must cover: what data is collected, why, how long it's kept, who it's shared with, how to request deletion. Use ICO template and adapt. Link from footer.
Critical Sonia + Dev 1–2 hrs
Add Terms of Service page
Defines Clip Services as a marketplace (not employer), limitation of liability, cancellation/refund policy, dispute resolution process. Protects Sonia legally. Use a free template from Termly or similar. Link from footer.
Critical Sonia + Dev 1–2 hrs
Draft and finalise Provider Agreement
1-page agreement every provider signs before going live: confirms self-employed status, 15% commission terms, right to remove listing, DBS requirement for in-home services, data handling consent, code of conduct.
Critical Sonia 2–3 hrs
Register with ICO as data controller
Legal requirement for processing personal data (names, addresses, phone numbers). Costs £40/year. Register online at ico.org.uk — takes 10 minutes.
Critical Sonia 15 min
Set up WhatsApp Business account with auto-replies
Download WhatsApp Business app. Set business name, description, hours, location. Configure auto-reply for out-of-hours messages. Set up quick replies for common responses (booking confirmation, provider notification).
Critical Sonia 30 min
Set up Monzo Business account
Separate all Clip Services income from personal finances. Receive customer payments, send provider payments, track commissions. Essential for tax reporting and scaling.
Critical Sonia 20 min
Recruit first 5 founding providers
Personal outreach — contact people Sonia already knows or find through local community groups. Need at minimum: 1 runner, 1 cleaner, 1 handyman, 1 dog walker, 1 beauty/other. Offer 0% commission for first month as founding incentive.
Critical Sonia 3–5 days
Replace sample provider data with real providers
Once founding providers are approved, replace the 8 sample entries in the providers JS array with real names, real bios, real rates, and real service lists. Remove fake reviews/ratings — start at 0 reviews or use "New Provider" badge.
Critical Dev 1 hr per provider

07Development Plan

This plan assumes Sonia is working on the business part-time alongside other commitments. Estimated 2–3 hours per day on Clip Services tasks. Development tasks assume access to Cursor for code changes.

Goal: Get all critical infrastructure in place so the site can accept real submissions and Sonia is legally protected.

Day 1–2 — Legal & Accounts
Set up the business backbone
Register with ICOico.org.uk — £40, 10 minutes. Get your registration number.
Set up Monzo BusinessDownload app, create business account, get payment links ready.
Set up WhatsApp BusinessBusiness profile, auto-replies, quick reply templates.
Draft Provider Agreement1-page document covering self-employed status, commission, removal rights, DBS.
Day 3–4 — Technical Fixes
Make the website actually work
Wire booking formConnect to Google Sheets or Vercel KV. Confirm data is captured and Sonia gets notified.
Wire provider application formSame backend. All 10 fields captured. Sonia receives email/WhatsApp alert on new application.
Replace WhatsApp placeholderUpdate all wa.me links with Sonia's real WhatsApp Business number.
Add Privacy Policy + TermsCreate two HTML pages, link from footer, add consent checkbox to forms.
Day 5–7 — Operations & Branding
Build the tracking system and brand presence
Create Google SheetsProvider tracker, booking tracker, financial tracker — all with the right columns and formulas.
Set up Instagram + TikTokBusiness profiles, first posts drafted, bio and link set.
Add SEO + OG tagsMeta description, og:image, favicon — site looks professional when shared.
Add Google AnalyticsTrack page views, form submissions, WhatsApp clicks.

Week 2 — Go Live

Goal: First 5 providers onboarded and first real bookings completed.

Day 8–10 — Supply Side
Recruit and onboard founding providers
Personal outreachContact 10 potential providers via WhatsApp. Target: 5 confirmed. Offer 0% commission for Month 1.
Vet and verifyID check via video call, DBS for in-home services, sign provider agreement, test WhatsApp response time.
Update website dataReplace sample providers with real provider cards. Real names, real bios, real rates. Start with "New Provider" badge (no fake reviews).
Deploy updatesPush to GitHub → Vercel auto-deploys. Test every form, every link, every flow end-to-end.
Day 11–14 — Demand Side
Get first customers and first bookings
Flyer drop50 flyers in Clippers Quays lobby, gym, lifts, mailboxes. QR code to website.
WhatsApp status blitzPost daily availability updates. "Cleaner available tomorrow 2–6pm." Creates urgency.
First 5 bookingsPersonally facilitate. Walk through the full flow: booking → payment → provider notification → job done → review request.
Social media launchInstagram: launch post + provider spotlights. TikTok: behind-the-scenes of building the marketplace.

Month 1 — Build Traction

Goal: 12 active providers, 50 total bookings, operations running smoothly.

Week 3–4
Scale supply and demand together
Grow to 12 providersApprove inbound applications. Proactively recruit beauty and tech providers to fill category gaps.
Google My BusinessRegister, optimise listing, ask first customers for Google reviews. Start appearing in local search results.
Launch referral programmeCustomer refers friend → both get £5 off. Track with referral codes in Google Sheets.
Purchase domainBuy clipservices.co.uk, connect to Vercel. Professional URL for all marketing materials.
End of Month 1 — Check-in
Review metrics and adjust
By end of Month 1, check: How many bookings completed? What's the repeat rate? Which categories get the most demand? Are providers responding fast enough? Is commission revenue covering the £40 ICO fee and flyer costs? Which marketing channel drove the most traffic?

Month 2–3 — Scale & Systemise

Goal: 25 providers, self-sustaining booking flow, revenue covers all costs.

Month 2
Platform becomes self-sustaining
25 active providersInbound applications should be flowing. Maintain 50–60% approval rate to protect quality.
B2B outreachVisit MediaCityUK offices. Offer bulk booking packages with monthly invoicing for businesses.
Featured placement revenueOffer top listing position to providers for £20–50/month. Second revenue stream.
Email backup systemSet up Mailchimp free tier. Collect emails. Send booking confirmations via email as WhatsApp backup.
Month 3
Automate and professionalise
Stripe Connect researchBegin Stripe Connect onboarding. Understand KYC requirements. Plan migration from manual to auto payments.
Provider WhatsApp CommunityCreate community channel for all approved providers. Post available jobs, share tips, celebrate milestones.
Hire VA / part-time assistantIf bookings exceed 50/week, Sonia needs help. Hire someone for booking confirmations and payment transfers.
Consider Ltd companyIf monthly revenue exceeds £1,000, forming a Ltd company becomes tax-efficient. Consult an accountant.

Month 4–6 — Automate & Expand

Goal: 50+ providers, Stripe payments live, expanding beyond Salford Quays.

Month 4–5
Payment automation and territory expansion
Stripe Connect liveAutomatic commission splits. Customer pays once, provider gets 85%, Sonia gets 15%. Zero manual work per transaction.
Provider dashboardSimple web page where providers can see upcoming bookings, earnings history, and profile stats.
Expand coverage areaTarget Trafford, Manchester city centre, Didsbury. Update marketing and provider recruitment for new zones.
Automated review systemPost-job WhatsApp message with review link. Reviews auto-publish to provider profile.
Month 6
Platform maturity milestone
50+ active providersFull category coverage. Multiple providers per category means customers always find someone available.
Monthly revenue £7,500+Commission-based income fully established. Business generates income while Sonia sleeps.
Loyalty programme"Clip Regulars" — 5% discount after 5 bookings. Increases retention and LTV.
Evaluate mobile appIf usage justifies it, begin React Native app planning. Until then, the website + WhatsApp combo works.
